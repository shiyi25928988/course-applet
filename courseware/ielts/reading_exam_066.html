In 1992, the United Nations Environmental Programme and the World Health Organisation (WHO) concluded that all of a sample of twenty megacities - places likely to have more than ten million inhabitants in the year 2000 - already exceeded the level the WHO deems healthy in at least one major pollutant. Two-thirds of them exceeded the guidelines for two, seven for three or more. Of the six pollutants monitored by the WHO - carbon dioxide, nitrogen dioxide, ozone, sulphur dioxide, lead and particulate matter - it is this last category that is attracting the most attention from health researchers. PM10, a sub-category of particulate matter measuring ten-millionths of a metre across, has been implicated in thousands of deaths a year in Britain alone. 💡 解题技巧 Tips & Strategies

  1. Skimming（略读）：先快速浏览全文，掌握文章大意和结构，注意每段的首句和尾句
  2. Scanning（扫读）：根据题干中的关键词（人名、地名、数字等）回原文定位答案
  3. 同义替换：雅思阅读90%的答案都是原文内容的同义改写，注意积累常见替换词
  4. 时间管理：建议每篇文章用时不超过20分钟，难题先跳过，先做简单题
  5. 拼写准确：注意答案的拼写和语法形式，单复数、时态错误都会导致失分
