B

The majority of the earth’s glaciers are located near the poles, though glaciers exist on all

continents, including Africa and Oceania. The reason glaciers are generally formed in high

alpine regions is that they require cold temperatures throughout the year. In these areas

where there is little opportunity for summer ablation (loss of mass), snow changes to

compacted fim and then crystallized ice. During periods in which melting and evaporation

exceed the amount of snowfall, glaciers will retreat rather than progress. While glaciers rely

heavily on snowfall, other climactic conditions including freezing rain, avalanches, and

wind, contribute to their growth. One year of below average precipitation can stunt the

growth of a glacier tremendously. With the rare exception of surging glaciers, a common

glacier flows about 10 inches per day in the summer and 5 inches per day in the winter.
