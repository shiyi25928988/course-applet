E The secret of the versatility of glass lies in its interior structure. Although it is rigid, and

thus like a solid, the atoms are arranged in a random disordered fashion, characteristic of a

liquid. In the melting process, the atoms in the raw materials are disturbed from their

normal position in the molecular structure; before they can find their way back to crystalline

arrangements the glass cools. This looseness in molecular structure gives the material

what engineers call tremendous “formability” which allows technicians to tailor glass to

whatever they need.
