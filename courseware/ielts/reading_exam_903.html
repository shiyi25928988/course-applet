Advertising isn’t the only way to link internal and external marketing. At Nike, a number of

senior executives now hold the additional title of “Corporate Storyteller.” They deliberately

avoid stories of financial successes and concentrate on parables of “just doing it,”

reflecting and reinforcing the company’s ad campaigns. One tale, for example, recalls how

legendary coach and Nike co-founder Bill Bowerman, in an effort to build a better shoe for

his team, poured rubber into the family waffle iron, giving birth to the prototype of Nike’s

famous Waffle Sole. By talking about such inventive moves, the company hopes to keep

the spirit of innovation that characterizes its ad campaigns alive and well within the

company.
