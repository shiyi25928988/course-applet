Shannon also laid the foundations of more efficient ways of storing information, by stripping

out superfluous (‘redundant’) bits from data which contributed little real information. As

mobile phone text messages like ‘I CN C U’ show, it is often possible to leave out a lot of

data without losing much meaning. As with error correction, however, there’s a limit beyond

which messages become too ambiguous. Shannon showed how to calculate this limit,

opening the way to the design of compression methods that cram maximum information

into the minimum space.
