Question 1 (8分)

主题：化学热力学 Thermodynamics

(a) Define standard enthalpy change of combustion. [2]

(b) Use the following data to calculate the enthalpy change of combustion of methane:

ΔHf(CH₄) = -75 kJ mol⁻¹

ΔHf(CO₂) = -394 kJ mol⁻¹

ΔHf(H₂O) = -286 kJ mol⁻¹ [4]

(c) Explain why the value calculated is likely to differ from the actual experimental value. [2]

📝 详细解答

(a)

The enthalpy change when one mole of a substance is completely burned in oxygen under standard conditions (298 K, 100 kPa).

(b)

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

ΔHc = ΣΔHf(products) - ΣΔHf(reactants)

ΔHc = [(-394) + 2(-286)] - [(-75) + 2(0)]

ΔHc = (-394 - 572) - (-75) = -966 + 75 = -891 kJ mol⁻¹

(c)

Standard conditions assume 298 K and 100 kPa, but combustion occurs at much higher temperatures.

Heat losses to surroundings occur in experiment.

Some incomplete combustion may occur.

Question 2 (9分)

主题：反应动力学 Kinetics (A2)

(a) Explain what is meant by the rate-determining step of a reaction. [2]

(b) The reaction 2NO + O₂ → 2NO₂ has the following mechanism:

Step 1: NO + NO ⇌ N₂O₂ (fast, reversible)

Step 2: N₂O₂ + O₂ → 2NO₂ (slow)

(i) Deduce the rate equation. [3]

(ii) State the overall order of reaction. [1]

(c) Explain why increasing temperature increases reaction rate using the Boltzmann distribution. [3]

📝 详细解答

(a)

The rate-determining step is the slowest step in a reaction mechanism.

It determines the overall rate of reaction, just as the slowest worker determines assembly line speed.

(b)(i)

Rate is determined by slow step (Step 2): Rate = k[N₂O₂][O₂]

But N₂O₂ is intermediate from Step 1: Kc = [N₂O₂]/[NO]² → [N₂O₂] = Kc[NO]²

Substituting: Rate = kKc[NO]²[O₂] = k'[NO]²[O₂]

(b)(ii)

Overall order = 2 + 1 = 3 (third order)

(c)

Boltzmann distribution shows number of molecules with particular energy.

Increasing temperature shifts distribution to higher energies.

More molecules have energy greater than activation energy.

More successful collisions occur when molecules collide.

Area above Ea increases significantly with temperature increase.

Question 3 (13分)

主题：有机化学 Organic Chemistry (A2)

(a) Describe and explain the acid-base properties of amino acids. [4]

(b) Draw the structure of a dipeptide formed from glycine and alanine. [3]

(c) Explain what is meant by the primary, secondary, and tertiary structure of proteins. [6]

📝 详细解答

(a)

Amino acids contain both acidic (-COOH) and basic (-NH₂) groups in the same molecule.

In solution, they form zwitterions: NH₃⁺-CH(R)-COO⁻

At low pH (acidic): -COO⁻ gains H⁺ → NH₃⁺-CH(R)-COOH (positive ion)

At high pH (alkaline): -NH₃⁺ loses H⁺ → NH₂-CH(R)-COO⁻ (negative ion)

They are amphoteric - can act as both acid and base.

(b)

Glycine: H₂N-CH₂-COOH

Alanine: H₂N-CH(CH₃)-COOH

Dipeptide: H₂N-CH₂-CONH-CH(CH₃)-COOH (peptide bond formed)

(c)

Primary: Linear sequence of amino acids, held by peptide bonds.

Secondary: Local folding into α-helix or β-pleated sheet, held by hydrogen bonds between C=O and N-H groups.

Tertiary: Overall 3D folding of polypeptide chain, held by disulfide bridges, ionic bonds, hydrogen bonds, and hydrophobic interactions between R groups.
