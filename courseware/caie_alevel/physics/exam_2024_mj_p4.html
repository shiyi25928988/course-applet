Question 1 (10分)

主题：简谐运动 Simple Harmonic Motion

(a) Define simple harmonic motion. [2]

(b) A mass-spring system oscillates with amplitude 5.0 cm and frequency 2.0 Hz.

(i) Calculate the maximum velocity. [3]

(ii) Calculate the maximum acceleration. [3]

(c) State where the velocity and acceleration are maximum and minimum in SHM. [2]

📝 详细解答

(a)

SHM is motion where acceleration is directly proportional to displacement from equilibrium, and directed towards equilibrium.

a ∝ -x (negative sign indicates opposite direction to displacement)

(b)(i)

ω = 2πf = 4π rad s⁻¹

v_max = ωA = 4π × 0.05 = 0.2π = 0.628 m s⁻¹

(b)(ii)

a_max = ω²A = (4π)² × 0.05 = 16π² × 0.05 = 7.90 m s⁻²

(c)

Velocity: maximum at equilibrium (x=0), minimum at extreme positions (x=±A)

Acceleration: maximum at extreme positions (x=±A), minimum at equilibrium (x=0)

Question 2 (10分)

主题：电磁感应 Electromagnetic Induction

(a) State Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction. [2]

(b) State Lenz's law and explain its physical significance. [3]

(c) A coil of 500 turns and area 0.01 m² rotates in a magnetic field of 0.2 T at 50 revolutions per second.

(i) Calculate the maximum induced emf. [3]

(ii) State two ways to increase the induced emf. [2]

📝 详细解答

(a)

Faraday's law: The induced emf is equal to the rate of change of magnetic flux linkage.

ε = -d(NΦ)/dt

(b)

Lenz's law: The direction of induced current opposes the change that caused it.

This is a consequence of conservation of energy - electrical energy cannot be created from nothing.

(c)(i)

ε_max = BANω

ω = 2π × 50 = 100π rad s⁻¹

ε_max = 0.2 × 0.01 × 500 × 100π = 100π = 314 V

(c)(ii)

1. Increase the number of turns in the coil

2. Increase the magnetic field strength

3. Increase the speed of rotation (any two)

Question 3 (10分)

主题：量子物理 Quantum Physics

(a) Explain what is meant by the photoelectric effect. [2]

(b) Explain why wave theory cannot explain the photoelectric effect. [3]

(c) Calculate the maximum kinetic energy of photoelectrons emitted when light of wavelength 400 nm shines on a metal with work function 2.0 eV. [5]

📝 详细解答

(a)

The photoelectric effect is the emission of electrons from a metal surface when electromagnetic radiation of sufficiently high frequency is incident on it.

(b)

Wave theory predicts that any frequency should cause emission given enough intensity, but there is a threshold frequency.

Wave theory predicts time delay for low intensity, but emission is instantaneous.

Wave theory predicts KE depends on intensity, but KE depends on frequency only.

(c)

Photon energy E = hc/λ = (6.63×10⁻³⁴)(3×10⁸)/(400×10⁻⁹) = 4.97×10⁻¹⁹ J = 3.11 eV

KE_max = E - Φ = 3.11 - 2.0 = 1.11 eV = 1.78×10⁻¹⁹ J
